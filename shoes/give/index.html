We Love GiveWell

Thank goodness for GiveWell. They are an independent non-profit started in 2007 by former hedge fund analysts. They are brutally demanding of the charities they recommend, and they are brutally demanding of themselves. (They have a “mistakes” tab on their site.) And they change their minds regularly as new studies and data are released.

Every charity makes mistakes — or at least they should, because aid is a chaotic, messy, human business. What matters is that their practices change once they discover the mistake.

Malaria

Against Malaria Foundation (AMF)
http://www.givewell.org/international/top-charities/AMF

Sleeping under a protective bed net / Photo: Arne Hoel / World Bank

In short: malaria is a cause of poverty.

Against Malaria Foundation has one simple goal: to eradicate the threat of malaria worldwide.

According to the World Health Organization, in 2011 there were 216 million cases of malaria. About 1 million of those people died, mostly children.

Those who survive the disease can’t work, and because they can’t work, they can’t afford medicine and often become trapped in poverty. One study found that a child who grew up malaria-free earned 50% more per year for his/her entire adult life. In short: malaria is a cause of poverty.

The simplest, most effective and economical way to fight malaria is to give out bed nets impregnated with insect repellent. Each net costs under $10 and with costs for distribution and administration, about $1600 will save a child’s life.

Cash to Those Who Need It

GiveDirectly
http://www.givewell.org/international/top-charities/give-directly

GiveDirectly transfers cash to households in the developing world via the M-PESA mobile phone-based payment service. It targets extremely low-income households and aims to deliver at least 90 cents directly to recipients for every $1.00 in total expenses.

GiveDirectly aims to deliver at least 90 cents directly to recipients for every $1.00 in total expenses.

Somewhat counter-intuitive — it actually works, if you just give the poorest people what they need… money. 67% of the money is used for home-improvement, 9% on livestock, 9% on “other” (like a motorcycle or school uniforms) and 4% is spent on food. All good.

DE-WORMING

Schistosomiasis Control Initiative (SCI)
http://givewell.org/international/top-charities/schistosomiasis-control-initiative/donate

SCI works with African governments to distribute de-worming medication to a mass scale to school-age children. SCI is GiveWell’s #3 charity.
